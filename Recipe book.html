DAN WAKE

Dan wake is a good meal made from scratch, and it is usually made from either of Guinea Corn/Beans Flour/Dried Cassava/Baking Flour or a combination of some, turned into dumpling, using the following ingredients;

Dan wake Recipe Quantity
Guinea Corn/Beans Flour/Dried Cassava/Baking Flour 1Kg
Eggs 3
Pepper powder 20g
Tomatoes 20g
Potash & Baobab powder 5g
Onions 2 bulbs
Cabbage 1g
Salt & Bouillon cubes 2 table spoon

How To Make Danwake;

When making Danwake, there is no need to rush. Each step counts therefore try to do each of the steps with care.

  1. Get a bowl, add in the flour (either of the first ingredients or a combination of some).
  2. Add in the baobab flour (otherwise called "kuka"). Use a spoon to stir until it is well mixed
  3. Dissolve the potash and add in the potash water bit by bit while stirring with a spoon to make the now wet flour dough. .
  4. Add the Ginger and Garlic and cook along with the Tomato paste for another two minutes.
  5. The mixture should not be too strong and not watery too. Make it not more than a cake or puff-puff mixture.
  6. Get your pot on heat, add 4 cups of water and allow to boil.
  7. When the water starts boiling, use your spoon and be fetching the dough, cutting into small chunks and throwing into the boiling water
  8. After you have added many chunks, use the spoon to stir them as you keep adding to avoid jamming them until the pot is filled.
  9. Allow to cook for 4 more minutes, sieve the water and wash 2 times with cold water.
  10. Peel and wash the tomatoes and onion and slice them into small pieces.
  11. Mix the Pepper powder, crush into it 1 seasoning cube
  12. Put the quantity you want to eat on a plate, add the pepper spices
  13. Get 4-5 tablespoon of vegetable oil, peel the boiled eggs and the sliced tomatoes and onions.
  14. Mix with a spoon and you are all set and ready to eat. Enjoy!

TUWO

Tuwo is a delicious, simple but satisfying recipe. It is made of some major grains like corn, millet, and rice.

Tuwo Recipe Quantity
Corn/Millet flour/Rice 1kg
Water 3ltr

How To Make Tuwo;

  • Put some water into a small pot and set it to boil on medium heat.
  • Add the corn/millet flour or rice and stir continuously until you get a thick and stretchy dough-like mix.
  • Add more corn/millet flour or the rice if you want it thicker.
  • Once the mixture is thick and stretchy, add a little water, cover the pot, reduce the heat and leave for 2 minutes.
  • Open and mix thoroughly until the mix is smooth, then mold into shapes as pleased.
  • Serve it hot on a plate and enjoy!

PS:To enjoy better, serve it with either of these soup varieties; Miyan Wake, Miyan Taushe , Miyan Zogale, Miyan Ridi, Miyan Tapasa and Miyan Shuaka! Learn more: Nigerian soup recipes

ALELE/MOI-MOI

This is a savory, fluffy, protein-rich food made of beans which is eaten alone or with bread as a snack, with rice as a meal or with ogi for breakfast or supper.

Alale Recipe Quantity
Black-eyed or brown beans 1kg
Pepper 1 tbsp
Onion 2 bulbs
Fish/Beef 2g
Bouillon cubes 4 pieces
Sardines/Crayfish 3 tbsp
Boiled Eggs 3
Ordinary water 3 ltrs
Salt 1/4 tbsp

How To Make Alale;

  1. First, soak the beans in cold water until they are soft enough to pulse and sieve to remove the fine outer covering or peel.
  2. Then drain and pour the soaked beans into a processor that would be used to grind or blend it until a fine paste is achieved.
  3. Starting to mix, add crayfish/sardines, beef, sliced boiled eggs, or a combination of these and other 'garnishes' to the paste to liven up the taste.
  4. Get a slanted pyramid-shaped cover, or a cylindrical shape, or cone shape or any of your targeted shape that would owe to the mold the paste is poured into prior to cooking.
  5. Add seasoned and garnished liquid into the cover and pour the paste.
  6. After packaging, place in a large pot about a tenth filled with water and arrnage, then close to allow the steam to be contained.
  7. Allow it to steam for like 45 minutes. If you notice that the water in the pot has dried up, carefully add more water.
  8. Carefully remove the Alale from the pot, uncover and place into plates, then add spices and oil, and enjoy!.

    9. PS:The process of making Alale can be made easy by grinding beans to flour also called Beans flour,
    This will reduce stress of peeling beans and washing multiple times, and all that would be needed is to mix the bean flour with water, add seasonings and salt to taste, fish, stocks, pawns, etc.
